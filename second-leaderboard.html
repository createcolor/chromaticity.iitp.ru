Photographer's choice

Small glossary of the photography terms used in the recent professional photographer's comments:

  • ‘Punch’, ‘punchy’: a characterization of the visual impact of an image, it is one of the most powerful factors that drives the mood of the users. This term shows to what degree the tones and the contrasts between light and dark grab the viewer’s attention. The following illustrative comparisons could help in understanding: it is a difference between a tune being played nicely on one piano and between the same tune being played by a concert orchestra - they are both technically correct, but the latter one is significantly more impactful; or, it is a difference between driving down the same road in an old VW Beetle versus driving down in a brand new Porsche. They both get you to your destination, but one ride is more visceral!

  • ‘Colour control’: a characterization of the quality of colour rendering. For example, when evaluating the colour control quality, in the second validation set there was an image with a green and yellow bin next to each other. The best entries rendered both colours well, but natural. The not so good entries either lacked colour here or had pushed the colours too far.
  • ‘Flat’, ‘flat rendering’: it is meant describe what appears as the lack of contrast between highlights and shadow values.
